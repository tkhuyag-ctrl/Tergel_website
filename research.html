Research & Interests

Exploring development economics and quantitative methods

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of development economics, applied microeconomics, and quantitative methods. I am particularly focused on understanding the mechanisms that drive economic development and the policies that can reduce poverty and inequality.

Development Economics

I am interested in understanding the factors that contribute to economic growth and poverty reduction in developing countries. This includes studying the role of institutions, human capital, infrastructure, and market access in shaping economic outcomes.

  • Poverty measurement and evaluation
  • Education and human capital development
  • Labor markets in developing economies
  • Microfinance and financial inclusion
  • Agricultural economics and rural development

Inequality & Social Mobility

Economic inequality is one of the defining challenges of our time. I am interested in understanding the causes of inequality, its consequences for social and economic outcomes, and the effectiveness of policies designed to promote more equitable growth.

  • Income and wealth inequality trends
  • Intergenerational mobility
  • Gender and racial economic disparities
  • Education as an equalizing force
  • Tax and transfer policies

Quantitative Methods

I am passionate about applying rigorous quantitative methods to economic questions. This includes econometric techniques for causal inference, computational methods for analyzing large datasets, and mathematical modeling of economic phenomena.

  • Causal inference and program evaluation
  • Regression analysis and panel data methods
  • Machine learning for economic prediction
  • Experimental and quasi-experimental design
  • Computational economics and simulation
