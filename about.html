About Me

My academic journey and aspirations

Academic Journey

I am currently pursuing a dual major in Economics and Applied Mathematics at Haverford College. My academic path has been shaped by a deep curiosity about how economic systems function and how mathematical tools can provide insights into complex social phenomena.

Through my coursework, I have developed a strong foundation in microeconomic and macroeconomic theory, econometrics, and advanced mathematical methods including real analysis, linear algebra, and probability theory. This interdisciplinary training allows me to approach economic questions with both theoretical rigor and empirical precision.

What Motivates Me

My interest in economics stems from a fundamental question: how can we create more equitable and prosperous societies? Growing up, I witnessed firsthand the impact of economic policies on communities and became fascinated by the potential of evidence-based research to inform better decision-making.

Mathematics provides the language and tools to formalize economic intuitions and test them rigorously. I am particularly drawn to the way quantitative methods can reveal patterns in data, helping us understand causal relationships and evaluate policy interventions. The combination of economic theory and mathematical precision offers a powerful framework for addressing development challenges and inequality.

Long-term Goals

My goal is to pursue graduate studies in economics, specializing in development economics and applied microeconomics. I aspire to contribute to academic research that informs policy decisions and helps build more inclusive economic systems.

In the long run, I envision working at the intersection of research and policy, whether in academia, international organizations, or research institutions. I am committed to producing work that bridges the gap between rigorous economic analysis and practical solutions to real-world problems, particularly in developing countries and emerging markets.

Beyond research, I am passionate about mentorship and education, and hope to inspire others to pursue careers that combine analytical rigor with social impact.

Current Focus

I am actively seeking research opportunities in development economics and building my technical skills in econometrics, data analysis, and computational methods. I am particularly interested in projects related to poverty, inequality, education, and labor markets in developing countries.
